Culture & Heritage

A Living
Tapestry

Thousands of years of Amazigh, Arab, Andalusian, and African traditions woven into one extraordinary culture.

🥁 Heritage Musician
Section 01

Gnaoua World Music Festival

Originating from the Gnawa spiritual traditions of sub-Saharan Africa, this festival was born in Essaouira as a celebration of trance music, healing rhythms, and cultural fusion.

Today it brings together global artists and preserves centuries-old Moroccan musical heritage.

🎶 Gnawa Music 🥁 Spiritual Rhythms 🌍 World Fusion 📍 Essaouira 🎭 Cultural Festival
🎶 Urban Sounds
Section 02

MAWAZINE FESTIVAL

Created in 2001 in Rabat, Mawazine was launched to position Morocco as a global cultural and musical hub. It was designed to bring together both international stars and Moroccan artists on the same stage, showing Morocco’s openness to world cultures while still celebrating its own identity.

Over the years, it has grown into one of the largest music festivals in Africa, hosting major global performers while keeping strong representation of local and Arab music traditions.

🎶 Live Music 🌍 Global Stage 📍 Rabat 🎭 Cultural Fusion
📚 Wisdom Beauty
Section 03

SALON DE LIVRE

The Rabat International Book and Publishing Fair (Salon du Livre) is one of Morocco’s most important cultural events. It was created to promote reading, publishing, and intellectual exchange in Morocco and the Arab world. Over time, it has become a major meeting point for writers, publishers, and readers from different countries.

A cultural space where books, ideas, and authors meet. It celebrates literature, education, and intellectual dialogue in Morocco.

📚 Literature ✍️ Authors 🌍 International 🏛️ Rabat 🎓 Education
🌟 Golden Silence
Section 04

MERZOUGA DESERT MUSIC FESTIVAL

The Merzouga Festival takes place near the Sahara dunes of Erg Chebbi. It was created to celebrate desert culture, Amazigh traditions, and nomadic heritage. It brings together music, poetry, and desert lifestyle experiences in one unique environment.

A desert cultural festival blending music, nature, and traditional Saharan life under the dunes of Merzouga.

🏜️ Desert Culture 🎶 Traditional Music 🐪 Nomadic Life 🌍 Amazigh Heritage
🌸 Floral Freshness
Section 05

M.GOUNA ROSE FESTIVAL

The Rose Festival of El Kelaa M’Gouna began in the Dades Valley, a region known for its vast rose fields. It was created to celebrate the annual rose harvest that takes place every spring when thousands of Damascus roses bloom in the valley. Over time, it evolved from a local harvest celebration into a national cultural festival that highlights Berber traditions, agriculture, and craftsmanship.

A vibrant spring celebration where nature, tradition, and local culture meet. It transforms the town of M’Gouna into a colorful festival filled with music, parades, rose products, and Amazigh heritage.

🌹 Rose Harvest 🌸 Spring Festival 🌍 Amazigh Culture 📍 M'Gouna 🧴 Natural Products
🎷 Jazz Mood
Section 06

JAZZABLANCA FESTIVAL

Jazzablanca Festival was created in Casablanca to celebrate jazz music and its fusion with other global genres. Over time, it evolved into one of Morocco’s leading urban music festivals, bringing together international jazz artists, soul musicians, funk bands, and contemporary performers.

A vibrant urban festival that blends jazz, soul, funk, and world music in the heart of Casablanca. It represents modern Morocco’s open and creative musical scene.

🎷 Jazz Fusion 🎶 Live Music 🏙️ Urban Vibes 📍 Casablanca 🌍 World Sounds
